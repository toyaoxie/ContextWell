Support

ContextWell Lab is an independent, research-led initiative. Support sustains the lab’s ability to operate, explore, and translate ideas into shared public value.

Support can take three complementary forms.

Donate
Support the lab’s continued existence and open work.
Sponsor
Support a specific research strand or project line.
Collaborate
Work together to explore, develop, and produce.

Donate

Donations provide flexible support that sustains the core operations of ContextWell Lab. They enable the lab to remain independent and to prioritise long-term human capacity over short-term optimisation.

Donations may contribute to:

  • maintaining and refining open conceptual frameworks
  • developing and maintaining experimental prototypes
  • producing openly accessible educational and research resources
  • supporting exploratory work outside conventional funding cycles

This form of support is especially suited to individuals who value open inquiry, public-interest research, and the careful development of ideas.

Make a donation

Sponsor

Sponsorship is designed for organisations, foundations, or programmes seeking to support a defined area of work within ContextWell Lab.

Unlike donations, sponsorship is typically directed toward a specific research question, project line, or development phase.

Sponsorship may support:

  • a defined research or conceptual programme
  • pilot studies or prototype development
  • evaluation, documentation, and dissemination activities
  • capacity-building initiatives aligned with shared priorities

Sponsorship arrangements are developed through dialogue and shaped by mutual goals, scope, and constraints.

Initiate sponsorship discussion

Collaborate

Collaboration is a central mode of operation for ContextWell Lab. The lab works with individuals and organisations interested in advancing well-being, judgment, and human oversight in complex and AI mediated environments.

Collaborations are research-informed, exploratory, and practice-oriented. They are designed to generate shared understanding, test ideas in real contexts, and translate insights into usable outputs.

Typical collaboration formats include:

  • research partnerships and co-authored studies
  • pilot projects in educational, health, or organisational settings
  • conceptual design and framework development
  • advisory work on well-being and human oversight
  • translation of research insights into tools, protocols, or learning systems

Collaborations may be project-based, time-limited, or exploratory, depending on mutual aims and constraints.

Propose a collaboration

Support for ContextWell Lab contributes to a broader effort to treat well-being and human oversight as foundational capacities for societies navigating technological complexity.
