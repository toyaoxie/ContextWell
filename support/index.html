Support ContextWell Lab

ContextWell Lab is a research-led initiative developing frameworks and prototypes that strengthen well-being and human oversight as trainable capacities in AI mediated environments.

This page offers three pathways, depending on your role and intent.

Academic and institutional

More “research institution” oriented: rigorous, aligned, and accountable.

This pathway is designed for universities, research groups, labs, foundations, and public institutions seeking to support research translation and capacity building around well-being and oversight in complex environments.

Typical support modes include:

  • institutional sponsorship of a defined strand of work
  • support for pilot studies, evaluation, and dissemination
  • co-designed seminars, modules, or training components
  • research partnerships and co-authored outputs

To start efficiently

Include: (1) institutional context, (2) the strand you want to support, (3) timeframe, (4) expected outputs or constraints.

Community support

More “public friendly”: clear, lightweight, and accessible.

Community support helps sustain open frameworks, educational materials, and early prototypes that remain publicly accessible. This pathway is suited to individuals who value open inquiry, practical well-being resources, and responsible AI related learning.

Community support may fund:

  • documentation and open resources (frameworks, diagrams, guides)
  • prototype iteration and maintenance
  • community-facing workshops or learning materials
  • translation of research into practical public formats
Donate Offer help or resources

If you cannot donate, sharing the lab’s work and pointing others to open resources is also meaningful support.

Applied partnerships

More “productised”: clearer scope, pilots, and adoption pathways.

This pathway is for organisations that want to translate research into practice. Partnerships focus on designing, piloting, and refining systems that strengthen well-being efficacy and oversight capacity under real-world constraints.

Typical partnership formats include:

  • pilot programmes in education, healthcare, or organisational environments
  • prototype or tool development (scenario based learning systems)
  • framework adoption and capacity-building integration
  • advisory support on oversight as a human capacity

What to include in a partnership inquiry

Include: (1) your setting, (2) the target users, (3) the context pressures you face, (4) what success would look like in 6–12 weeks.
